Beanie Babies



One of the 90's most frienzied fads was hoarding small pellet filled, and often tie dyed toys in the shape of bears, moose, and other, woodland animals.

While most beanie babies sold for $5 a piece, limited edition collectables such as "Humphrey" the camel, sold for over $1,000. Beanie baby theft was rampant, grown adults spent hours searching thrift stores and flea markets searching for valuable toys to resell, and couples fought over their collections in divorce court.

Beanie baby production ceased on December 31, 1999 at 11:59pm. For those who still collect, there is always Ebay.
